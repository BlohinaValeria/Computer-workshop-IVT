Pembroke Welsh Corgi

  • Origin: Wales, UK
  • Tail: Short or docked
  • Temperament: Outgoing, playful, affectionate
  • Coat: Medium, straight, water-resistant
  • Famous for: Queen Elizabeth II’s favorite breed

Cardigan Welsh Corgi

  • Origin: Wales, UK
  • Tail: Long, bushy
  • Temperament: Loyal, smart, reserved with strangers
  • Coat: Medium, dense, weatherproof
  • Distinctive: Larger, rounded ears and longer body

Breed History

  • Both breeds originated as herding dogs in Wales over 1,000 years ago.
  • Pembrokes and Cardigans were recognized as separate breeds in the 1930s.
  • Known for their intelligence, agility, and affectionate nature.

Care Tips for Corgi Owners

Diet

Feed a balanced, high-quality dog food. Watch their weight—Corgis are prone to obesity!

Exercise

Daily walks and playtime are essential. Corgis are energetic and need both physical and mental stimulation.

Grooming

Brush their double coat weekly. Expect more shedding during seasonal changes.

Health

Regular vet checkups, vaccinations, and dental care are important. Watch for hip dysplasia and back issues.

Fun Facts About Corgis

  • Queen Elizabeth II has owned more than 30 Corgis!
  • Corgis were bred to herd cattle and are surprisingly fast.
  • Their name means "dwarf dog" in Welsh.
  • They have a "fairy saddle" marking on their back, according to legend.
  • Corgis are known for their big personalities and expressive faces.
  • Both Pembroke and Cardigan Corgis have short legs but are very agile.
  • Corgis are social media stars thanks to their cuteness!
